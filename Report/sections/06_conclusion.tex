\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
In summary, in this project I have developed a flexible python pipeline to generate data for grayscale continuous Fourier patterned surfaces meant for light-trapping.
I have carefully validated convergence of said data. I have developed three different surrogate models that allow solving problems of the same input space orders of magnitude faster than classical methods, and accelerates the inverse design process.
These surrogate neural networks predicts the absorptance curves of unseen light-trapping designs to the real optical performance with an average error of $\approx 2.5\%$. By utilizing insighful loss functions, I have trained models that bypass common traps in broadband nanophotonic topology optimization.
I have shown the potential of surrogate solvers both as a complement to, but also as a replacement to conventional light-trapping design.
